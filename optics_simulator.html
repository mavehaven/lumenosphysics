Physics Explanation

Ray Optics Principles

Ray optics, or geometrical optics, describes light propagation in terms of rays. This simulator demonstrates how light rays interact with various optical elements based on fundamental physics principles.

Snell's Law: \( n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2) \)
Governs how light bends when passing between materials with different refractive indices.

Lens Equation

The lens equation relates the object distance, image distance, and focal length of a lens:

\( \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} \)
Where \( f \) is focal length, \( u \) is object distance, and \( v \) is image distance.

Total Internal Reflection

When light travels from a higher refractive index to a lower one, beyond a certain critical angle, all light reflects internally:

\( \theta_c = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right) \)
Critical angle calculation where \( n_1 > n_2 \).

Optical Physics Principles

Light as a Ray

Ray Approximation: Light is modeled as straight-line rays to simplify analysis of optical systems.

Applications: Essential for designing imaging systems, lasers, and optical instruments.

Snell's Law

The Equation: $$ n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2) $$

Key Insight: Governs how light bends when moving between media, critical for minimizing optical aberrations.

Lens Physics

Convex Lenses: Focus parallel rays to a focal point using: $$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} $$

Concave Lenses: Spread parallel rays to form virtual images for vision correction.

Practical Applications

Imaging Systems: Cameras, telescopes, and microscopes rely on precise lens configurations.

Laser Systems: Require accurate ray control for collimation and beam shaping.

AR/VR Displays: Use lens arrays and waveguides to create immersive experiences.
