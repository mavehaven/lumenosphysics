Key Concepts & Equations

Newton's Laws of Motion

First Law (Inertia)

An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net force.

ΣF = 0
Second Law (Force & Acceleration)

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it.

F = ma
Third Law (Action-Reaction)

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

F₁₂ = -F₂₁

Energy & Work

Work

The work done by a force is the product of the force and the displacement in its direction.

W = F·d
Kinetic Energy

The energy possessed by an object due to its motion.

KE = ½mv²
Potential Energy

The energy possessed by an object due to its position or configuration.

PE = mgh

Historical Milestones

1905: Special Relativity

Einstein publishes "On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies," introducing special relativity.

1915: General Relativity

Einstein completes general relativity, explaining gravity as curved spacetime.

1919: Experimental Confirmation

Eddington's expedition observes light bending during solar eclipse, confirming general relativity.

Fundamental Principles

Special Relativity Postulates

First Postulate

The laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames.

Second Postulate

The speed of light in vacuum is constant for all observers.

Special Relativity Equations

Lorentz Factor

Relativistic correction factor

γ = 1/√(1-v²/c²)
Time Dilation

Moving clock runs slower

Δt = γΔt₀
Length Contraction

Moving objects appear shorter

L = L₀/γ
Mass-Energy Equivalence

Einstein's most famous equation

E = mc²

General Relativity Principles

Equivalence Principle

Gravitational and inertial mass are equivalent. The effects of gravity are indistinguishable from acceleration.

Curved Spacetime

Mass and energy curve spacetime; curved spacetime tells matter how to move.

Einstein Field Equations

Relating matter to spacetime curvature

Gμν = 8πG/c⁴ Tμν

Experimental Evidence

Light Bending

Starlight bends around the Sun during eclipses, confirming spacetime curvature.

Gravitational Time Dilation

GPS satellites must account for both special and general relativistic time effects.

Gravitational Waves

LIGO's detection of gravitational waves from merging black holes.
